### What We Know About Post-Hypoxic Brain Injury
- Hypoxic-ischemic injury preferentially affects areas with high metabolic demand: hippocampus, basal ganglia, cerebral cortex, cerebellum
- Processing speed impairment is one of the most common and persistent cognitive sequelae
- The developing brain has greater neuroplasticity than adult brain — recovery window extends longer
- Evidence supports multi-modal intervention combining medical, cognitive, and lifestyle approaches

## Recovery Tracks (Research Framework)

### Track 1: Medical Foundation
- Current neuropsychological evaluation status
- Neuroimaging (has post-injury MRI been done? fMRI for functional mapping?)
- Cardiology clearance for physical activity and exercise-based interventions
- Medication review (any neuroprotective or cognitive-enhancing medications being considered?)
- Sleep study (sleep disruption is common post-hypoxic injury and impairs recovery)

### Track 2: Cognitive Rehabilitation
- **Formal cognitive rehabilitation therapy (CRT)** — structured, therapist-led
- **Processing speed-specific interventions** — targeted exercises and strategies
- **Compensatory strategies** — tools and techniques to work around deficits while recovery continues
- **Cognitive training platforms** — evidence level varies; identify which have clinical support for brain injury populations

### Track 3: Therapeutic Modalities
Map evidence level for each in context of hypoxic brain injury recovery:
- Occupational therapy (cognitive-focused)
- Speech-language pathology (cognitive-communication)
- Neurofeedback / EEG biofeedback
- Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT)
- Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS)
- Acupuncture and traditional Chinese medicine
- Craniosacral therapy
- Vision therapy (if visual processing affected)
- Music therapy / rhythmic auditory stimulation
- Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR)
- Somatic experiencing / body-based therapies
- Art therapy

### Track 4: Educational & Academic Support
- Neuropsychological evaluation → IEP or 504 plan documentation
- Processing speed accommodations (extended time, reduced workload, note-taking support)
- Executive function coaching
- Tutoring with brain injury awareness
- School liaison and advocacy strategies

### Track 5: Lifestyle & Neuroplasticity Support
- **Exercise**: Aerobic exercise evidence for neuroplasticity (BDNF upregulation)
- **Sleep**: Sleep hygiene and its role in neural repair
- **Nutrition**: Anti-inflammatory diet, omega-3s, specific micronutrients with evidence
- **Social engagement**: Role of social interaction in cognitive recovery
- **Stress reduction**: Cortisol impact on recovery; stress management approaches
