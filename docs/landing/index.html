The Problem

Every AI tool gives you an answer and forgets why you asked. For regulated professionals, it's worse: cloud AI means sending client data to someone else's server. Attorney-client privilege. HIPAA. Fiduciary duty. For a law firm, that's not a privacy risk — it's a malpractice risk.

25 million regulated professionals in the US alone need AI but cannot use the cloud. They're buying Mac Minis, hiring contractors, and duct-taping open-weight models together. Nobody is selling them a governed solution.

The Solution

Palette is an SDK for Humans. It doesn't just answer questions — it classifies what kind of problem you're facing, retrieves governed knowledge, and stores every decision in structured memory that compounds over time.

Chat history remembers what you said. Palette remembers what you decided, why, and what kind of problem it was. That's the difference between a conversation and a judgment trail.

1

Ask a question

Palette classifies what kind of problem you're facing before searching for answers. Not keyword matching — structured understanding. 121 problem types, each with governance metadata.

2

Safe external research

When local knowledge isn't enough, Palette queries Perplexity for public legal research — but only after stripping all client identifiers. Party names, case numbers, dollar amounts, strategy language: all blocked. Your client data never leaves your machine.

3

Your judgment compounds

Every decision is stored in structured memory. Tomorrow's questions connect to today's answers. The system remembers what kind of problem this is, what you decided, and why. After a month, Palette knows your practice better than any cloud chatbot ever could.
