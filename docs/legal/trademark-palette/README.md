## The Refusal

**Section 2(d) Likelihood of Confusion** with U.S. Registration No. 7613647:

- **Their mark**: PALETTE (standard characters)
- **Their services**: "Software as a service (SAAS) services featuring software for use in project management of digital artists"
- **Our mark**: PALETTE (stylized with horizontal line and star)
- **Our original services**: "Software as a service (SAAS) services featuring software for project management"

The examiner's argument: the marks are highly similar (same word) and the services are legally identical because our broad "project management" encompasses their "project management of digital artists."

## Our Response (Filed July 1, 2026)

### 1. Amended Services Description

Changed from:
> Software as a service (SAAS) services featuring software for project management.

Changed to:
> Software as a service (SAAS) services featuring software using artificial intelligence for project management of AI agent configurations, classification and routing of data queries, and machine learning operations.

**Why this wording**: 
- Keeps "project management" to frame the amendment as a narrowing (not a substitution), which is required under 37 CFR 2.71(a)
- Uses accepted USPTO ID Manual terms ("artificial intelligence," "machine learning," "classification")
- Describes what Palette actually does: AI agent governance, ontology-based classification, knowledge architecture
- Clearly distinguishes from "project management of digital artists"

### 2. Legal Argument (DuPont Factors)

Argued four DuPont factors from *In re E.I. du Pont de Nemours & Co.*, 476 F.2d 1357 (CCPA 1973):

**Factor 2 — Dissimilarity of Services** (strongest argument):
- Our services: AI agent configuration management, data query classification, ML operations
- Their services: project management for digital artists
- No functional overlap. Different nature, purpose, and function.
- Cited: TMEP 1207.01(a)(vi)

**Factor 3 — Dissimilarity of Trade Channels**:
- Ours: AI/ML developer platforms, technical conferences, enterprise software channels
- Theirs: creative industry channels, design platforms, digital art communities
- No overlap in trade channels.

**Factor 4 — Sophistication of Consumers**:
- Our consumers: AI/ML engineers, data scientists, technical operations teams
- Highly sophisticated purchasers unlikely to confuse an AI operations platform with a digital artist PM tool
- Cited: *In re N.A.D., Inc.*, 754 F.2d 996 (Fed. Cir. 1985); TMEP 1207.01(d)(i)

**Factor 1 — Similarity of Marks**:
- Both include "PALETTE" but ours is stylized (design mark with horizontal line and star)
- Where services are sufficiently dissimilar, even identical marks may coexist
- Cited: *Shen Manufacturing Co. v. Ritz Hotel Ltd.*, 393 F.3d 1238 (Fed. Cir. 2004)
