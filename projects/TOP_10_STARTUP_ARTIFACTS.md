# Top 10 Startup Artifacts

**Author**: codex.implementation
**Date**: 2026-03-29
**Status**: Proposal

## Purpose

Define the top startup artifacts the unified system should be able to generate from day one.

These are the artifacts that make the product feel immediately useful.

## Selection Criteria

Each startup artifact should be:
- high-frequency
- compressive
- low-friction
- valuable without long setup
- easy to refine conversationally

## The Top 10

### 1. What Changed Brief

Purpose:
- get the user back into context fast

Includes:
- top changes since last session
- what matters now
- what needs attention

Why it matters:
- this is the best re-entry artifact

### 2. Decision Board Snapshot

Purpose:
- orient the user immediately

Includes:
- knowns
- unknowns
- blockers
- open decisions
- next moves

Why it matters:
- this is the core mission-state artifact

### 3. Daily Brief

Purpose:
- provide a morning orientation artifact

Examples:
- funding brief for Rossi
- market brief for investor

Why it matters:
- makes the system feel alive and contextual

### 4. Next Action Plan

Purpose:
- move the user from ambiguity to motion

Includes:
- top 3-5 next actions
- who owns them
- what is blocked

Why it matters:
- users often want motion, not analysis

### 5. Evidence Gap Summary

Purpose:
- show what is missing and why it matters

Includes:
- missing evidence
- priority
- who resolves it
- what it unlocks

Why it matters:
- knowledge gaps are one of the system’s strongest differentiators

### 6. Recommendation Note

Purpose:
- provide a concise answer to “what do you think we should do?”

Includes:
- recommendation
- rationale
- assumptions
- risks

Why it matters:
- supports real decisions without requiring a huge artifact

### 7. Stakeholder Update Draft

Purpose:
- turn internal state into external communication

Examples:
- investor note
- board update
- advisor summary
- partner email

Why it matters:
- this is direct labor savings

### 8. Meeting Brief

Purpose:
- prepare the user for a live conversation

Includes:
- context
- key questions
- decisions needed
- evidence to bring

Why it matters:
- very high utility
- easy to trigger by voice

### 9. Source / Evidence Pack

Purpose:
- gather supporting material into one bundle

Includes:
- source list
- short summaries
- citations
- unresolved gaps

Why it matters:
- improves trust and follow-through

### 10. Plan Draft

Purpose:
- convert a goal into a structured plan

Examples:
- 30-day plan
- grant readiness plan
- regulatory response plan
- weekly execution plan

Why it matters:
- one of the most obvious “AI created something useful” moments

## Startup Artifact Ordering

On first launch, the system should bias toward:

1. What Changed Brief
2. Decision Board Snapshot
3. Daily Brief
4. Next Action Plan

Those four are the best “re-entry + value” loop.

## Segment Notes

### Rossi / Founder / Operator

Best startup artifacts:
- Decision Board Snapshot
- Evidence Gap Summary
- Next Action Plan
- Stakeholder Update Draft

### Oil Investor / Executive

Best startup artifacts:
- Daily Brief
- What Changed Brief
- Recommendation Note
- Meeting Brief

## Product Rule

If the system starts with a blank chat box, it is underperforming.

It should start by offering or generating one of the startup artifacts above.

## Recommendation

Make startup artifact generation a first-class workspace configuration setting.

That way each workspace can choose:
- default startup artifact
- fallback startup artifact
- role-specific startup sequence
