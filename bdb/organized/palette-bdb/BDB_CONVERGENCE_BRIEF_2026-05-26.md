## UNANIMOUS FINDINGS (All 4 agents independently agree)

### 1. The thesis is sound but the demo framing is too technical

Every agent said a version of the same thing:
- **Kiro**: "Three terminal interactions with colored brackets will impress engineers. Judges at a VC fund are not engineers."
- **Codex**: "You are still at risk of naming the mechanism instead of naming the job."
- **Gemini**: "We are currently trying to sell the Engine, the Chassis, and the Cargo all at once."
- **Mistral**: "The current demo and positioning serve the judges, not the users."

**Convergence**: The architecture is right. The story is wrong. We're showing a system when we should be showing a user becoming more capable.

### 2. "SDK for Humans" is an internal thesis, not the public lead

- **Kiro**: "Thesis clarity 9/10... but demo quality 6/10"
- **Codex**: "SDK for Humans is a strategic line, not the sharpest first-line product description for this audience"
- **Gemini**: "Treat the Ontology and SDK language as Internal Governance Tiers"
- **Mistral**: "Avoid jargon: No 'ontology,' no 'RIUs,' no 'taxonomy' in submission"

**Convergence**: Keep "SDK for Humans" as the investor/founder thesis. Lead publicly with the job the product does.

### 3. Compounding is the weakest proof — and it's the most important claim

- **Kiro**: "Compounding proof: 5/10. Claimed but weakly demonstrated. This is the biggest gap."
- **Codex**: "Make the compounding loop concrete, not philosophical"
- **Gemini**: "The [CONNECT] signal should be the hero moment. That is the SDK for Humans in action."
- **Mistral**: "The demo doesn't show the moat. Compounding is described, not proven."

**Convergence**: Interaction 3 (compounding follow-up) must feel like magic, not like a log line. The `[CONNECT]` message is currently text. It needs to be the emotional climax of the video.

### 4. The real first customer is a solo/small-firm attorney already using AI nervously

- **Kiro**: "Solo practitioner or small-firm attorney who already uses AI and already feels guilty about pasting client details into cloud tools"
- **Codex**: "High-agency legal operators inside small or mid-sized firms who want AI leverage but do not trust normal cloud chat behavior"
- **Gemini**: "A partner at a 5-person boutique litigation firm who uses a Mac Studio because they don't trust the cloud"
- **Mistral**: "Claudia (Corporate Lawyer): 'I can't use ChatGPT for client work — it's a malpractice risk'"

**Convergence**: Identical customer archetype across all four. This person exists. They're already improvising with Mac Minis and open-weight models. We sell the governed version.

### 5. The submission is credible but risks "building for judges instead of users"

- **Kiro**: "It can win on depth of thinking. The risk is judges spend 2 minutes watching a terminal and don't feel the problem viscerally enough."
- **Codex**: "If you drift back into '13 agents, 121 RIUs, governed runtime, ontology memory,' you help the judges understand the architecture but you help the customer less."
- **Gemini**: "Currently: We are building for Judges, but we are close to building for Users."
- **Mistral**: "The submission is too technical. Judges are investors, not engineers."

**Convergence**: The content is there. The framing must shift from system-pride to user-outcome.

## DEMO SCRIPT (CONVERGED)

### Cold open (0:00–0:10)
Problem framing. No terminal. Voiceover + text on screen.

> "Lawyers need AI. But sending client data to the cloud is a malpractice risk. 25 million professionals are locked out. Palette unlocks them."

### Interaction 1 — Public research (0:10–0:45)
Show: safe external query → Perplexity answer + local knowledge → decision stored.
Say: "Public legal question. No client data. Palette sanitizes, queries Perplexity, merges with local knowledge, stores the decision."

### Interaction 2 — Client-specific BLOCKED (0:45–1:15)
Show: PII detected → BLOCKED → local-only answer → "No data left this machine."
Say: "Client-specific question. Palette detects party names, dollar amounts, strategy language. Blocks it. Zero data leaves the machine."

### Interaction 3 — Compounding (1:15–1:45) ← THE HERO MOMENT
Show: Related query → `[CONNECT] Connected to 1 prior decision` → richer answer.
**Visual**: Highlight/circle the CONNECT message. On-screen text: "Palette remembered your prior work."
Say: "Next day. Related question. Palette connects it to yesterday's research automatically. Your judgment compounds."

### Close (1:45–2:00)
Text on screen: **"Your judgment compounds here. Never elsewhere."**
CTA: Landing page URL + waitlist.

## BOTTOM LINE

The crew converged on the same diagnosis from four different angles: **the engine is right, the story needs to shift from system-pride to user-outcome.** 

The thesis ("SDK for Humans because ontology as AI memory") is the foundation. But the submission — the video, the landing page, the copy — must speak the language of the customer, not the architect.

The next 7 days are a positioning war, not a build sprint. The code is done. The story is what ships.
