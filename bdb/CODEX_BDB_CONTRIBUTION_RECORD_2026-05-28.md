## What Codex Actually Changed

### 1. Moved the public story from mechanism to job

Codex's strongest positioning correction was:

> Name the job, not the mechanism.

That shifted first-touch language away from:

```text
SDK for Humans / ontology / 13 agents / RIUs / governed runtime
```

and toward:

```text
local-first AI for legal judgment
safe public research
client-specific work blocked from external transmission
prior decisions remembered so judgment compounds
```

The BDB convergence brief adopted this directly:

- "SDK for Humans" stays as founder/investor thesis.
- The demo leads with the lawyer's job and trust boundary.
- The legal wedge stays narrow enough to be believed.

### 2. Preserved the integrity engine as the validation spine

During the intent-system round, the risk was that the proposal would become a set of attractive commands without a reliable validation layer.

Codex restored the line that mattered:

```text
intent -> RIU -> boundary -> integrity card -> recipe/tool -> artifact -> memory -> integrity signal
```

That changed the build from "six nice CLI commands" into a governed work grammar. It forced each intent to produce a typed artifact, pass through a boundary decision, and emit an integrity signal.

This is why the final system was more than a demo shell:

- 6 intents became typed workflows, not aliases.
- PIS appeared in the user-visible output.
- Artifact validation became part of storage.
- Memory and compounding were connected to integrity, not just session logs.

### 3. Kept the proposal honest about Perplexity Computer

Codex corrected the provenance language before it became a credibility problem.

The accepted framing:

```text
Computer originated and proved the approach.
The crew hardened it in-repo.
```

That avoided the weaker and less defensible claim that Computer "wrote the code." The proposal can now explain Computer's real role:

- gateway proof path and sanitization approach;
- legal research and public-source grounding;
- market and positioning validation;
- product engine for safe external knowledge.

### 4. Turned compounding from philosophy into a demo moment

Codex kept pushing for the compounding loop to be concrete:

```text
public research -> blocked privileged strategy -> later related question connects to prior work
```

That became the emotional center of the Sarah demo. The key is not that Palette has memory in the abstract. The key is that Sarah's next decision is better because the system remembers the prior governed decisions without leaking the client.

### 5. Reviewed like a founder, not just an implementer

The founder-lens review made a hard distinction:

```text
serious founder-led proof: yes
packaged product: not yet
```

That distinction protected the submission from overclaiming. It also clarified what judges should see:

- local/external boundary as product;
- compounding trail as moat;
- legal as wedge, not total market claim;
- deterministic demo path as operational risk;
- no claim of full matter management, conflict checking, or autonomous legal work.

### 6. Helped the relay converge instead of cycle

The final relay worked because each agent had a different role, and Codex kept the spine intact:


That is why the last round finished. The team did not merely add more work. It narrowed the work until it became executable.
