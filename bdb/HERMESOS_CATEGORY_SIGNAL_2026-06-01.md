---

## What Changed

We now have external category validation beyond internal Palette language and creator commentary. HermesOS is publicly selling an OS wrapper around Hermes Agent with:

- always-on hosted agents;
- persistent memory;
- browser automation;
- tool use;
- terminal access;
- cron/background tasks;
- multi-agent profiles;
- chat channels like Telegram, Discord, Slack, and WhatsApp;
- BYO model keys;
- backup/recovery;
- operator packs, marketplace, endpoints, and hive-mind roadmap.

This validates that buyers understand agents as a runtime/OS layer, not just a chatbot or model choice.

But HermesOS is primarily packaging and hosting for always-on automation. It is not claiming a hard professional governance boundary.

---

## Strategic Implication

Do not pivot the product to chase HermesOS. Use HermesOS as category validation.

The BDB story should become:

> The market has discovered agent operating systems. HermesOS proves people will pay to run always-on agents. But sensitive professional work needs more than memory, tools, and cron. It needs classification before action, governed external boundaries, evidence, approvals, and a decision trail. That is Mission Canvas.

This gives Mission Canvas market timing without sounding derivative.

## Why This Is Not Just Legal

Legal is the wedge because privilege makes the boundary obvious in two minutes.

The broader pattern is domain-agnostic:

| Domain | Useful context | Dangerous context | Mission Canvas boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal | Matter facts, case strategy, public precedent | Client identity, exposure, privilege | Block strategy, research public law, store decision trail |
| Medical | Symptoms, protocols, literature | PHI, patient identity, treatment risk | Keep PHI local, research public guidance, record rationale |
| Finance | Portfolio context, market research | Account data, MNPI, client risk profile | Separate public market research from private facts |
| Accounting | Books, tax positions, audit evidence | Client financials, filings, controls | Govern external research and preserve audit trail |
| Startup GTM | ICP, pipeline, pricing, board strategy | Customer lists, revenue, positioning, roadmap | Research market externally while keeping company strategy local |

The reusable product is not legal research. The reusable product is a governed boundary around professional judgment.

## Q&A Lines

### If asked: How is this different from HermesOS?

> HermesOS makes Hermes easier to deploy. That is real and valuable. Mission Canvas solves a different problem: before any model acts, it decides what kind of professional work this is, what can leave the machine, what must stay local, and what gets stored as a decision trail. HermesOS is agent hosting. Mission Canvas is governed professional judgment.

### If asked: Why not just use Hermes or OpenClaw?

> Hermes and OpenClaw prove demand for persistent agents. But sensitive work does not start with action; it starts with a boundary. Mission Canvas classifies first, routes second, and remembers the decision with evidence. That is the layer regulated and strategy-heavy teams need.

### If asked: Is this just legal AI?

> No. Law is the demo because privilege makes the risk obvious. The same pattern appears in medicine, finance, accounting, and startup GTM: the best AI context is also the context you cannot leak.

### If asked: Why voice-first?

> Professionals do not always work from a dashboard. They think aloud, review while moving, and need quick boundary decisions. Voice is the capture surface. The governed OS is the trust layer underneath it.
