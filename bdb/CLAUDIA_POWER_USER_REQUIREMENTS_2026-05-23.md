# Claudia Power User Requirements
## How Palette Starts With One Expert And Expands To A School
**Date**: 2026-05-23
**Purpose**: define what a Claudia-class power user needs from Palette so her personal use becomes transferable institutional capability

---

## The Core Idea

Claudia should not experience Palette as:

- a chatbot
- a generic AI assistant
- a pile of disconnected tools

She should experience it as:

- a thinking partner for institutional design
- a memory system for her repeated judgment
- a framework for turning her methods into reusable school capability

That means the product path is not:

1. give the whole school AI
2. hope they figure it out

The path is:

1. help Claudia use Palette to improve her own real workflow
2. capture what works as structured skills, artifacts, and decision patterns
3. turn those into repeatable school-level workflows for other teachers and leaders

This is the power-user wedge.

---

## Claudia's Real Role In The System

Claudia is not just a teacher-user.
She is the first high-leverage operator.

She already does the kinds of work Palette is meant to support:

- curriculum architecture
- assessment design
- AI integration planning
- cross-stakeholder communication
- translating expertise into transferable systems

So the product does not need to teach her how to think.
It needs to help her:

- structure what she already knows
- bridge into technical and AI implementation domains
- preserve her reasoning over time
- turn her methods into shareable institutional assets

That is the right frame.

---

## The Product Goal

For Claudia, the goal is:

> Palette should help her improve her own workflow first, then convert the strongest parts of that workflow into reusable school capability.

This is a two-stage product motion:

### Stage 1 — Personal leverage

Palette helps Claudia herself work better.

### Stage 2 — Institutional transfer

Palette helps Claudia turn what works into a system that other people can use.

If Stage 1 fails, there is nothing to transfer.
If Stage 2 fails, the value stays trapped inside one exceptional person.

The product must do both.

---

## What Claudia Needs In Stage 1

## 1. A project-based operating model

Claudia does not need a blank chat box.
She needs a workspace organized around real ongoing work:

- High School Program Design
- Lingua Viva
- AI Integration Strategy
- ARON / adaptive learning
- Structural Coherence Framework

Palette should let her enter through a project, not through an empty prompt.

### Why this matters

This makes the system feel like a continuity environment rather than a conversation reset.

---

## 2. Structured problem framing

Claudia often sees the whole system at once.
That is a strength, but it also creates overload.

Palette should help her narrow each session into:

- current project
- current decision
- current blocker
- current audience
- smallest next move

### Why this matters

She does not need simplification.
She needs sequence.

---

## 3. Confidence-building through provenance

Claudia is an expert user.
She will trust the system more when it shows:

- where an idea came from
- what sources support it
- what prior decisions it connects to
- what assumptions are being made

She is not a user who wants the system to hide complexity.
She wants the system to surface the right kind of complexity.

### Why this matters

The expert power user becomes a champion only if the system makes them more confident, not less.

---

## 4. Bridging into technical domains without forcing technical identity

Claudia does not need to become “the AI expert.”
She needs help extending her educational and architectural expertise into AI implementation.

Palette should help her answer questions like:

- What is the smallest safe AI pilot?
- What tool is appropriate here?
- What governance constraints matter?
- What does leadership need to understand?
- What can teachers actually adopt?

### Why this matters

This is the exact “expert outside her domain” use case already present in the repo.

---

## 5. Reusable artifact creation

Claudia’s real output is not just answers.
It is artifacts:

- proposals
- frameworks
- assessment structures
- teacher-facing playbooks
- leadership briefs
- parent communication

Palette should help produce and improve those artifacts in forms she can reuse.

### Why this matters

A power user compounds through artifacts, not only interactions.

---

## 6. Visible memory of prior work

Claudia should be able to return and see:

- what she decided before
- what evidence supported that decision
- what changed
- what related work already exists

She should feel:

- continuity
- compounding
- architectural memory

not simply “another AI session.”

---

## What Claudia Needs In Stage 2

Once Palette is genuinely useful to Claudia personally, the next job is institutional transfer.

This is where the system becomes more than a personal tool.

## 1. Skill extraction from repeated success

When Claudia uses Palette repeatedly on the same class of work, the system should start turning those patterns into shareable skills.

Examples:

- how to structure an AI classroom proposal
- how to design a CEFR-aligned progression
- how to present structural coherence work to leadership
- how to separate student privacy concerns from tool experimentation

These should become reusable objects, not only remembered conversations.

### Product requirement

Palette should help distinguish:

- one-off output
- repeated workflow
- reusable method

That is where institutional value begins.

---

## 2. Transferable playbooks

The system should help Claudia turn her own high-quality work into:

- teacher playbooks
- leader briefs
- implementation recipes
- onboarding packets

That means the product must support not just `thinking with Claudia`, but `packaging Claudia’s method for others`.

### Why this matters

This is how one power user becomes organizational capability.

---

## 3. Audience-specific output layers

Claudia works across multiple audiences:

- herself as designer
- school leadership
- teachers
- families

Palette should help her transform the same underlying reasoning into different surfaces for different audiences.

For example:

- internal design notes
- executive summary for leadership
- teacher rollout guide
- family-facing explanation

### Why this matters

Institutional adoption fails when one smart person cannot translate their work across audiences.

---

## 4. Governance and privacy clarity

For school use, especially around children, the system must make clear:

- what stays local
- what can be externalized
- what requires consent
- what should never be stored or shared

Claudia cannot be expected to carry all of that mentally every time.
Palette has to encode it.

### Why this matters

If the power user does not trust the boundary, they cannot responsibly champion the system.

---

## 5. School-level memory, not only personal memory

Eventually the system should move from:

- Claudia’s judgment memory

to:

- shared school capability memory

That does not mean flattening everything into one shared vault.
It means building the right layers:

- private working memory
- team-sharable workflows
- institution-level approved practices

### Why this matters

The system should scale beyond the person without erasing the person.

---

## The Claudia Adoption Flywheel

Here is the product motion in its strongest form:

### Step 1 — Claudia uses Palette on real work

Not hypothetical prompts.
Real school architecture problems.

### Step 2 — Palette improves her workflow

It helps her:

- frame
- retrieve
- compare
- remember
- communicate

### Step 3 — Repeated wins are captured

What worked becomes:

- prior decisions
- structured artifacts
- reusable skills
- trusted patterns

### Step 4 — Claudia reuses and refines those patterns

Now the system compounds for her personally.

### Step 5 — The strongest patterns become transferable

They get turned into:

- teacher workflows
- leadership briefs
- program design methods
- school playbooks

### Step 6 — The school begins using Claudia-shaped capability

At this point, Palette is no longer just helping one expert.
It is helping one expert’s best methods become institutional infrastructure.

This is the right product flywheel.

---

## What Must Be True For Claudia To Become The First Real Champion

Claudia becomes a true Palette champion if all of these become true:

### 1. The system saves her time without lowering quality

Not just faster output.
Faster high-quality output.

### 2. The system respects her expertise

It does not patronize her or flatten her work into generic AI language.

### 3. The system increases her confidence in adjacent technical domains

She feels more capable, not more dependent.

### 4. The system remembers what matters

She can come back later and build from prior work.

### 5. The system helps her teach others

She can turn her methods into shared school capability.

If all five are true, she is not just a user.
She becomes the first credible internal proof of the whole thesis.
