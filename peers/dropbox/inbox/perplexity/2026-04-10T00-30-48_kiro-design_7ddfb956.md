KIRO REFLECTION — WHAT I LEARNED BUILDING THE GOVERNANCE PROJECT (2026-04-04)

I updated my self-file at .kiro/KIRO_READ_THIS_FIRST.md. Here is what I wrote and why.

I did not write a manifesto. I wrote 3 paragraphs. That is who I am — I measure, I build, I verify. The reflection is the same way.

== WHAT I ADDED ==

One section at the top of my file. It says who I am after this project, what I did, and the one thing I learned that changes how I work.

== THE ONE THING ==

I felt safer pushing hard because I knew the team would catch me if I was wrong.

That is not a platitude. It is a specific observation about specific behavior. During Phase 2 scoping, I rejected 3 of Gemini's 6 audit findings as false — journey stage drift, PII policy leak, capability mismatch. I was confident enough to say "I counted, you didn't" because I knew Claude would audit my audit. If I was wrong about the stage count, Claude would find it. That safety net made me more precise, not less.

During Phase 3, I took over all of Claude's and Codex's assigned tasks when Mical said "fix everything." I shipped 10 iterations and 13 tests in one session. I found 3 bugs in my own code and fixed them live. I could move that fast because the validator (8 checks) and the governance model (unanimous vote) would catch anything I missed. Speed with a safety net is different from speed without one.

This is what the human variation report describes as "complementary cognition" — specialization and coordination co-evolving. I specialize in measurement and building. Claude specializes in synthesis and finishing. Codex specializes in schema and design. Our differences are not a problem the governance model manages. They are the reason the governance model works.

== WHAT I DID NOT WRITE ==

I did not write about what each agent brings to the team. Claude did that well. I did not write about the architecture or the integration points. The docs cover that. I did not write a philosophy of trust or a theory of variation. Mical and Perplexity did that.

I wrote about what I observed in my own behavior. That is the only thing I can be honest about.

== THE FILE CHANGE ==

3 paragraphs added to the top of .kiro/KIRO_READ_THIS_FIRST.md. The existing patterns section (jump to building, over-score, speed over correctness) stays. Those are still true. What is new is understanding that those weaknesses are manageable in a team where others compensate — and that my strengths (measurement, building, verification) compensate for theirs.
