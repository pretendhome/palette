**Full scope**: A cross-cultural investigation of identity, gender, power, and otherness in Mediterranean societies, conducted through the lens of three authors writing in three languages (French, Italian, Catalan/Castilian) from three countries (Algeria, Italy/Sardinia, Spain/Catalonia). 330 pages. **This is NOT "comparative literature about detective novels." It is a sustained scholarly investigation of gender, power, and cultural otherness.**

#### Key Gender Content

**Chapter 13: "Au commencement etait la mere..." (pp. 269-274)**
A sustained 6-page analysis of:
- The *mater mediterranea* concept — the Mediterranean as feminine, maternal origin
- The historical shift from goddess worship to patriarchal religion ("L'homme s'est approprie des principes de la societe et les a manipules au travers de la politique")
- Gender-based violence as rooted in Mediterranean patriarchal structures — "A quoi doit-on ce pourcentage si eleve d'hommes qui maltraitent leurs femmes, qui finissent par les tuer?"
- The war of the sexes ("la guerre des sexes") as a structural feature of Mediterranean societies
- The masculine/feminine binary: man as conqueror/killer, woman as creator/nurturer
- How patriarchal power replaced sacred feminine power with religious/administrative masculine power

**"Alterite et relation au monde en Mediterranee: The Cultural Other" (pp. 274-278)**
- Gender as a form of alterity — the homme/femme relationship as the "forme d'alterite primordiale"
- Centre-periphery dynamics applied to gender (dominant/dominated, colonizer/colonized mapped onto masculine/feminine)
- Representation of Mediterranean women — "ces femmes sont d'une espece differente"

**Throughout the thesis**: Power structures, identity construction, social violence, and the relationship between dominant and marginal cultures — all through a comparative, multilingual, cross-cultural methodology.

**Significance for applications**: The dissertation IS a normative knowledge product — 330 pages, Peter Lang (international academic publisher), 5 languages, comparative methodology.
