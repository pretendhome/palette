## GAP 1: NGL Pricing & Midstream Financial Frameworks

The original research covers upstream and downstream thoroughly but omits NGLs (natural gas liquids) — critical for midstream companies and integrated producers.

### NGL Components & Pricing

**Mont Belvieu, Texas** is the NGL pricing hub for the US. Almost every gallon of propane, butane, ethane, and natural gasoline in the US market is priced off Mont Belvieu benchmarks. [Source: OPIS — LPG + NGL Pricing](https://www.opis.com/commodities/lpg-ngl/)

The 5 NGL components and their typical product mix:

| Component | Chemical | Typical Mix % | Primary Use | Pricing Benchmark |
|-----------|----------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Ethane** (C2) | C₂H₆ | ~35-40% | Petrochemical feedstock (ethylene crackers) | Mont Belvieu TET |
| **Propane** (C3) | C₃H₈ | ~30% | Heating, petrochemical feedstock, exports | Mont Belvieu TET |
| **Normal Butane** (nC4) | C₄H₁₀ | ~10% | Gasoline blending, petrochemical feedstock | Mont Belvieu non-TET |
| **Isobutane** (iC4) | C₄H₁₀ | ~5% | Alkylation (high-octane gasoline) | Mont Belvieu TET |
| **Natural Gasoline** (C5+) | C₅H₁₂+ | ~15-20% | Gasoline blending, diluent for heavy crude | Mont Belvieu non-TET |

[Source: EIA — Prices for Hydrocarbon Gas Liquids](https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/hydrocarbon-gas-liquids/prices-for-hydrocarbon-gas-liquids.php)

### NGL Pricing Dynamics

- **Ethane**: Tracks natural gas prices as a floor (ethane rejection — when processors leave ethane in the gas stream because extracting it isn't economic). Since late 2017, ethane prices decoupled upward from natural gas as new petrochemical crackers and ethane export terminals created demand. Ethane price is bracketed between natural gas (floor) and propane (ceiling). [Source: EIA](https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/hydrocarbon-gas-liquids/prices-for-hydrocarbon-gas-liquids.php)

- **Propane**: Decoupled from crude oil pricing in 2011-2012 as US production surged. Now driven by domestic heating demand (winter), petrochemical demand, and massive export volumes. **US propane exports averaged a record 1.8 million b/d in 2025** — the highest since data collection began in 1973. [Source: EIA — NGL Exports 2025](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=67387)

- **Butane (normal)**: US exports reached a record ~535,000 b/d in 2025, up 9% YoY. Driven by gasoline blending demand and petrochemical use. [Source: EIA](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=67387)

- **Frac spread**: The difference between NGL prices and natural gas price — the economic incentive for gas processors to extract NGLs rather than reject them. A wider frac spread = more profitable midstream processing.

### Midstream MLP Financial Frameworks

Midstream companies — especially MLPs (Master Limited Partnerships) — use different financial metrics than upstream E&Ps:

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Distribution Coverage Ratio (DCR)** | Distributable Cash Flow / Distributions Paid | >1.2x (current avg: 1.7-1.8x) | Below 1.0x = paying out more than earned = unsustainable. Forecasted 1.8-1.9x over next 5 years. |
| **Debt/EBITDA** | Total Debt / Adj. EBITDA | 3.0-4.0x target (current avg: 3.7x investment grade) | Down from 5.6x in June 2021. Most midstream names target 3x-4x. |
| **Fee-Based Revenue %** | Fee revenue / Total revenue | >80% preferred | Higher = more insulated from commodity price swings |
| **Distributable Cash Flow (DCF)** | EBITDA - Maintenance Capex - Interest - Taxes | Growing YoY | MLP equivalent of free cash flow |
| **Growth Capex** | Capital for new projects (pipelines, plants) | Context-dependent | High growth capex = future EBITDA but near-term cash drain |

[Source: Global X ETFs — Midstream & MLP Insights](https://www.globalxetfs.com/articles/midstream-and-mlp-insights-natural-gas-the-fuel-behind-midstream-resilience-in-2025/), [ETF Trends — MLP Leverage Ratios 2025](https://www.etftrends.com/energy-infrastructure-content-hub/2025-midstream-mlp-leverage-ratios-signal-flexibility/), [AInvest — MLP Dividend Coverage 2024](https://www.ainvest.com/news/examining-2024-midstream-mlp-dividend-coverage-stability-evolution-2505/)

**Key midstream companies for investors:**
- **Enterprise Products Partners (EPD)**: Largest US midstream MLP. NGL pipelines, fractionation, storage. >80% fee-based.
- **MPLX (MPLX)**: Marathon Petroleum's midstream arm. Adj. EBITDA from NGL rose 19% YoY in Q1 2025 due to higher frac spreads. DCR: 1.5x. Debt/EBITDA: 3.3x. [Source: MPLX IR](https://ir.mplx.com/CorporateProfile/press-releases/news-release/2025/MPLX-LP-Reports-Third-Quarter-2025-Financial-Results-Announces-Increase-to-Quarterly-Distribution-of-12-5/default.aspx)
- **Williams Companies (WMB)**: Natural gas processing and transportation. Transco pipeline system.
- **Kinder Morgan (KMI)**: Pipeline giant. Largest US natural gas pipeline network.

## GAP 2: EIA API Practical Integration

### API Architecture

The EIA Open Data API v2 is a RESTful JSON API. All endpoints follow this pattern:

```
https://api.eia.gov/v2/{route}/{sub-route}/data/?api_key={YOUR_KEY}&{parameters}
```

[Source: EIA API Documentation](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/documentation.php)

### Authentication

- **Free API key** — register at [eia.gov/opendata](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/)
- Key passed as query parameter: `?api_key=YOUR_KEY`
- No OAuth, no bearer tokens — just the key

### Key Petroleum Endpoints

| Data | Route | Frequency |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Crude oil spot prices** | `/v2/petroleum/pri/spt/data/` | Daily |
| **Weekly petroleum status** | `/v2/petroleum/sum/sndw/data/` | Weekly (Wed) |
| **Crude inventories (Cushing)** | `/v2/petroleum/stoc/wstk/data/` | Weekly |
| **Natural gas Henry Hub spot** | `/v2/natural-gas/pri/fut/data/` | Daily |
| **US crude production** | `/v2/petroleum/crd/crpdn/data/` | Monthly |

### Example Request (WTI Spot Price)

```
GET https://api.eia.gov/v2/petroleum/pri/spt/data/
  ?api_key=YOUR_KEY
  &frequency=daily
  &data[0]=value
  &facets[product][]=EPCWTI
  &start=2026-03-01
  &end=2026-03-30
  &sort[0][column]=period
  &sort[0][direction]=desc
```

### Response Format (JSON)

```json
{
  "response": {
    "total": 22,
    "data": [
      {
        "period": "2026-03-28",
        "product": "EPCWTI",
        "product-name": "Cushing, OK WTI Spot Price FOB",
        "value": 97.42,
        "units": "$/bbl"
      }
    ]
  }
}
```

### Rate Limits

- Max **5,000 rows** per request (300 for XML)
- Throttling based on key usage, current demand, and IP
- No published hard limit on requests/minute, but heavy use triggers throttling
- **Recommendation**: Cache daily — prices update once/day for spot, once/week for inventories

[Source: EIA API v2.1.0 Documentation](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/documentation/APIv2.1.0.pdf), [EIA API Browser](https://www.eia.gov/opendata/browser/petroleum/pri/)

### Integration Pattern for the Workspace

```
Startup:
  → Fetch WTI, Brent, Henry Hub spot prices (3 API calls)
  → Fetch latest weekly inventory summary (1 call)
  → Cache with TTL: prices = 4 hours, inventory = 24 hours

On "what changed" query:
  → Fetch yesterday + today prices, compute delta
  → Compare Cushing inventory to 5-year average

On "daily brief" artifact:
  → Pull all cached data, format into brief template
```

## GAP 3: Dallas Fed Energy Survey — Break-Even Data

The **Dallas Fed Energy Survey** is the authoritative quarterly survey of oil and gas executives, conducted by the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas. It is now available on FRED. [Source: FRED Adds Dallas Fed Energy Survey Data](https://news.research.stlouisfed.org/2026/01/fred-adds-dallas-fed-energy-survey-data/)

| Metric | Value | Trend |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Business Activity Index** | +21.0 (up from -6.2 in Q4 2025) | Expansion |
| **Average break-even to profitably drill (all basins)** | **$66/bbl** | Up from $65 in Q1 2025 |
| **Permian Basin break-even** | **$67/bbl** | Up from $65 YoY |
| **Break-even range across basins** | $62–$70/bbl | Rising with inflation and service costs |
| **WTI price expectation (2-year)** | $73/bbl | |
| **WTI price expectation (5-year)** | $79/bbl | |
| **Henry Hub year-end 2026** | $3.60/MMBtu | |
| **Henry Hub 2-year expectation** | $4.03/MMBtu | |
| **Henry Hub 5-year expectation** | $4.42/MMBtu | |

[Source: Dallas Fed Energy Survey Q1 2026](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/surveys/des/2026/2601)

### Basin Focus (Q1 2026 respondents)

- **82%** of respondents focused on **Permian Basin**
- **29%** focused on **Eagle Ford**
- **29%** focused on **Utica (Ohio)**

### AI Impact on Break-Evens

- **38%** of large E&P executives expect AI to cut breakeven costs by **$0.01–$1/bbl** over next 5 years
- **70%** of smaller E&Ps expect **no break-even reductions from AI**

[Source: JPT/SPE — Dallas Fed Shale Survey](https://jpt.spe.org/dallas-fed-survey-finds-cost-pressures-weighing-on-us-shale-outlook)

### Why This Matters for the Workspace

The Dallas Fed survey is a **leading indicator** — it captures executive sentiment and forward guidance before it shows up in production data. For the oil investor workspace:
- **Proactive nudge**: "Dallas Fed Q1 survey shows Permian break-evens rose to $67/bbl — your position in Diamondback has 29% margin at current WTI."
- **Scenario input**: Break-even data feeds the scenario memo (what WTI level makes portfolio companies uneconomic?)
- **Survey frequency**: Quarterly (March, June, September, December) — mark as calendar catalyst

### Key Design Decisions (from Addepar/Stanford research)

- **97% of professional investors have a formal memo template** — the workspace must produce structured, not freeform, outputs
- **78% require memo approval before investing** — the recommendation note is a governance artifact, not just information
- Investment memos serve as "valuable windows into the inner workings of an organization's decision-making" — they must be traceable and auditable
- The memo process is typically: **Screen → Write → Critique/Stress-Test → Score → Approve/Reject**

[Source: Addepar Research Brief](https://info.addepar.com/rs/232-KUI-215/images/addepar-investment-memos.pdf), [Institutional Investor — How Memos Can Improve](https://www.institutionalinvestor.com/article/2bwoy30j6qec76g7e4idc/portfolio/memos-are-an-inescapable-part-of-the-investment-process-heres-how-they-can-improve)

## GAP 5: Voice UX Patterns for Financial Data

### Google's VUI Design Principles (applied to financial context)

**Rule of Three**: Present no more than 3 items in a spoken list. "You have three flagged items: Brent dropped four-forty-five overnight, the EIA report comes out Wednesday, and a FERC order on Mountain Valley Pipeline landed yesterday." Not five items — three. [Source: Google Design — Rule of Three](https://design.google/library/rule-of-three)

**Cognitive load in voice**: "Speech is inextricably bound to the passage of time... the longer someone holds the floor, the more brainwork they're imposing on the listener." Financial voice output must be compressed. A morning brief should be 45-60 seconds, not 3 minutes. [Source: Google Design — VUI Principles](https://design.google/library/speaking-the-same-language-vui)

### Speaking Financial Numbers — Best Practices

| Data Type | Written | Spoken (Best Practice) |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| Oil price | $97.42/bbl | "Ninety-seven forty-two a barrel" (drop the dollar sign and units — context is established) |
| Price change | -$4.45 | "Down four forty-five" (lead with direction) |
| Percentage | +3.2% | "Up three point two percent" |
| Large numbers | $640B market cap | "Six hundred forty billion market cap" |
| Ratios | 2.3x Net Debt/EBITDA | "Two point three times leverage" (simplify the metric name) |
| Dates | 2026-03-30 | "March thirtieth" (never ISO format in speech) |
| Tickers | XOM, CVX | Spell out: "Exxon" and "Chevron" (never speak ticker symbols — speak company names) |

### Voice Interaction Design Rules for the Workspace

1. **Lead with delta, not absolute** — "Brent's down four forty-five" not "Brent is at one-oh-one forty"
2. **Speak company names, not tickers** — "Your Diamondback position" not "Your FANG position"
3. **Round aggressively in speech** — "About sixty-seven dollars" not "$66.82" — precision in voice is noise
4. **Slow down for action items** — TTS rate should drop for "Do you want me to..." prompts
5. **Pause before numbers** — Brief 200ms pause before delivering a number lets the listener's brain switch to numeric processing mode
6. **Confirm large actions** — "That would be a sell recommendation on your Permian Resources position. Should I draft the note?" — always confirm before artifacts with financial consequence
7. **Three items maximum per spoken list** — per Google's Rule of Three. If there are 5 nudges, speak the top 3 and say "plus two more — want the full list?"
8. **Cite sources in speech** — "According to EIA data" or "Per the Dallas Fed survey" — maintains credibility without being academic
9. **Use relative time** — "The rig count dropped for the third straight week" not "The Baker Hughes rig count for the week ending March 28, 2026 was 592"
10. **End every brief with a choice** — "Want the scenario memo, the full brief, or should I flag anything for tomorrow?"

[Source: Google Design — Speaking the Same Language](https://design.google/library/speaking-the-same-language-vui), [Coval.dev — Voice AI Stack for Financial Industry](https://www.coval.dev/blog/how-to-optimize-your-voice-ai-stack-for-the-financial-industry)
