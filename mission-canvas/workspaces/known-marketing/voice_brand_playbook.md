## Brand Voice Guidelines

### Who Known Sounds Like

Known sounds like your most honest friend who happens to be great at dating. Not a tech company. Not a dating coach. Not a therapist. A friend who says what everyone's thinking but nobody's saying.

### Tone

- **Warm but direct** — say it like you mean it, no corporate padding
- **Confident without bragging** — the product speaks for itself (80% go on dates)
- **Playful but never cheesy** — humor comes from honesty, not puns
- **Anti-hype** — understate, don't oversell. Let the numbers do the work

### Vocabulary

**Use freely:**
- voice, conversation, real, actual, meet, date, person, connect, honest, genuine
- "your actual voice" — this is the core differentiator
- "one person at a time" — scarcity creates value
- numbers: "80%", "26 minutes", "24 hours" — specifics build trust

**Use carefully:**
- AI, technology, app — only when explaining how it works, never as the selling point
- matching, compatibility — Known doesn't "match" you, it introduces you
- better, best, revolutionary — show don't tell

**Never say:**
- swipe (the enemy)
- algorithm (sounds cold)
- AI-powered (sounds like every other app)
- disrupting (cringe)
- find your person / find the one (pressure)
- no more ghosting (negative framing — say "24-hour windows" instead)

### The One-Liner

> "You talk. We listen. You meet one real person."

### The Longer Version

> "Known replaces your dating profile with your actual voice. 26 minutes of real conversation — not typing, not swiping, not performing. Then we introduce you to one person at a time. 80% of our intros turn into actual dates. Because when people hear each other first, they actually want to meet."

## 2-Minute Investor/Partner Pitch Flow

### Opening (15 sec)
"The dating app market is $12 billion — and it's broken. Bumble lost 16% of paying users last year. Tinder is losing Gen Z. The entire industry is built on a mechanic that people hate: swiping."

### Problem (20 sec)
"Here's what swiping actually does: it turns people into products. You judge someone in half a second based on photos. Then you message for days without meeting. Then someone ghosts. The average dating app user spends 90 minutes a day swiping and goes on one date a month. That's not dating. That's a second job."

### Solution (25 sec)
"Known replaces the entire profile-and-swipe model with voice. When you join Known, you have a 26-minute voice conversation. Not with a chatbot — with our AI that actually listens to how you talk, what you care about, what makes you laugh. Then we introduce you to one person at a time. One. With a 24-hour window to set up a date."

### Traction (20 sec)
"In our SF beta: 80% of introductions turn into real dates. That's not a typo. 80%. The industry average is somewhere between 5 and 15%. Our users aren't swiping 90 minutes a day — they're going on dates. We have a waitlist and we're growing through word of mouth alone."

### Business (20 sec)
"We raised $9.7 million from Forerunner, NFX, and Pear. We've tested a per-date pricing model at $30 — users pay when they actually meet someone, not for the privilege of swiping. Our unit economics improve with every match because voice data gets better at introductions over time."

### Close (15 sec)
"Match Group is a $30 billion company built on a mechanic people hate. We're building what dating should have been all along. We're looking for partners who understand that the next generation of dating isn't about more options — it's about better ones."

## Content Calendar Framework

### Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Content Type | Platform | Goal |
|-----|-------------|----------|------|
| Mon | Data point ("80% stat") | Twitter/X | Credibility |
| Tue | User story (anonymized) | TikTok/Reels | Emotional connection |
| Wed | "Swipe culture" commentary | TikTok/Reels | Problem awareness |
| Thu | Behind-the-scenes (voice AI) | Instagram Stories | Transparency |
| Fri | "Weekend date" prompt | All platforms | Engagement |
| Sat | Silence | — | Intentional scarcity |
| Sun | Founder voice note | Instagram/TikTok | Authenticity |

### Content Rules

1. **Never post more than once per day** — Known is about quality, not volume
2. **Every post must be speakable** — if it doesn't sound natural read aloud, rewrite it
3. **No stock photos ever** — real people, real moments, or abstract/minimal design
4. **Numbers over adjectives** — "80% go on dates" beats "the best dating app"
5. **Respond to every comment for the first 1000 followers** — community before scale
