#### ⚡ QUICK START (5 minutes)

**You'll produce**: A shared-vs-specific map for 2 brands showing what AI capabilities are shared and what's brand-specific.

**Steps**:
1. Pick 2 brands (real or realistic) that could share AI capabilities.
2. List 5 AI capabilities they both need (e.g., customer service bot, content generation, search).
3. For each, classify: shared (same model, same data), customized (same model, different config), or independent (different everything).
4. For the shared items, identify the governance question: who decides when the shared capability changes?
5. Write it as a clean shared-vs-specific map.

**⚠ Common mistakes** (watch for these):
- Sharing everything to save cost — brands have different voices, policies, and audiences.
- Sharing nothing to avoid coordination — duplicates effort and creates inconsistency.
- No governance for shared capabilities — one brand changes something and breaks it for another.
- Ignoring data boundaries — sharing a model is fine, sharing customer data across brands may not be.

#### 🔨 APPLIED (15-30 minutes)

**You'll produce**: A work-ready multi-brand strategy with capability mapping, governance model, and migration plan.

**Steps**:
1. Map all AI capabilities across your brands. Which exist today? Which are planned?
2. For each capability, analyze: shared infrastructure (yes/no), shared model (yes/no), shared data (yes/no), shared policy (yes/no).
3. Design the governance model: who owns shared capabilities? How are changes approved? What's the escalation path when brands disagree?
4. Identify quick wins: capabilities that are duplicated today and could be consolidated immediately.
5. Identify risks: capabilities that look shareable but have hidden brand-specific requirements.
6. Write a migration plan: what to consolidate first, what to leave independent, and what to evaluate later.
7. Write the complete strategy.

**✓ Check your work** — standard 6-point check with confidence delta.

#### 🏗️ PRODUCTION (30-60 minutes)

**You'll produce**: A portfolio-grade multi-brand AI architecture with federated governance, shared platform design, brand customization layers, and measurement framework.

**Steps**:
1. Design the shared platform: what infrastructure, models, and tools are shared across all brands?
2. Design the customization layer: how does each brand configure the shared platform for their needs?
3. Build federated governance: central standards + brand-level autonomy. Define what's centrally controlled vs locally decided.
4. Add measurement: how do you track whether the shared strategy is actually saving cost and improving quality?
5. Design for new brands: when a new brand joins the portfolio, what's the onboarding process?
6. Plan for divergence: when should a brand break away from the shared platform? What triggers that decision?
7. Document the complete architecture.
