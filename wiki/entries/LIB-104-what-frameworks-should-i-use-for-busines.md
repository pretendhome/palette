# What frameworks should I use for business model design?

Use these proven frameworks for business model design:

## Definition

Use these proven frameworks for business model design:

**1. Business Model Canvas** (Osterwalder)
- 9 building blocks: Value proposition, customer segments, channels,
  customer relationships, revenue streams, key resources, key activities,
  key partnerships, cost structure
- Best for: Startups and new ventures
- Time: 1-2 hours to complete
- Output: One-page visual business model

**2. Value Proposition Canvas** (Osterwalder)
- Customer profile: Jobs to be done, pains, gains
- Value map: Products/services, pain relievers, gain creators
- Best for: Product-market fit validation
- Time: 30-60 min to complete
- Output: Customer-value alignment map

**3. Lean Canvas** (Maurya)
- Focuses on: Problem, solution, key metrics, unfair advantage, channels
- Best for: Lean startups and MVPs
- Time: 20-30 min to complete
- Output: One-page lean business model

**4. Strategic Framework** (Porter's 5 Forces + SWOT)
- Porter's 5 Forces: Supplier power, buyer power, competitive rivalry,
  threat of substitutes, threat of new entrants
- SWOT: Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats
- Best for: Established businesses and competitive strategy
- Time: 2-3 hours to complete
- Output: Competitive positioning analysis

**When to use which**:
- New startup → Lean Canvas or Business Model Canvas
- Product validation → Value Proposition Canvas
- Strategic planning → Porter's 5 Forces + SWOT
- Comprehensive plan → Combine multiple frameworks

**Rossi Mission example**:
- Used Business Model Canvas for revenue streams (7 streams)
- Used Value Proposition Canvas for artist value (50/50 split)
- Used SWOT for competitive positioning (scarcity economics)
