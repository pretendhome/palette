# How do I design and deliver effective training sessions for AI system adoption?

RIU-103 defines training design in four layers. Layer 1 — Audience Segmentation: segment trainees by role and skill level. A store associate needs different training than a data analyst. Design separate tracks for: end users (task-focused, 30-60 minutes), power users (configuration and customization, 2-4 hours), and administrators (ops, monitoring, troubleshooting, half day). Never deliver one-size-fits-all training. Layer 2 — Exercise-First Design: structure each session as 20% explanation, 80% hands-on exercises using real or realistic data. Every exercise should mirror an actual workflow the trainee will perform daily. Include a "break it" exercise where trainees intentionally trigger an error and learn the recovery process — this builds confidence with failure modes. Layer 3 — Common Mistakes Prevention: compile the top 10 mistakes from pilot users and address each explicitly in training. Example: "Many users try to ask the system X. Here is why that does not work and what to do instead." This is more effective than feature tours. Layer 4 — Follow-Up Support: training is not a one-time event. Schedule a 30-minute follow-up session 1-2 weeks after initial training to address real-world questions. Create a FAQ document from training Q&A. Establish a support channel (Slack, Teams) for ongoing questions. Measure training effectiveness by task completion rate, not satisfaction score.
