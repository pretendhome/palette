# What should a business plan document include?

Standard business plan structure (25-50 pages):

## Definition

Standard business plan structure (25-50 pages):

**1. Executive Summary** (2 pages)
- Business concept and value proposition
- Market opportunity (size, trends, segments)
- Competitive advantage (differentiation)
- Financial highlights (revenue, costs, funding)
- Team overview (founders, key hires)
- Funding requirements (if applicable)
- Go/no-go recommendation

**2. Decision Framework** (3 pages) - Palette addition
- 3-5 ONE-WAY DOORS with gradient descent options
- Human confirmation checkboxes
- Game theory analysis (when each option wins)

**3. Company Description** (3-5 pages)
- Mission and vision
- Legal structure (LLC, C-corp, nonprofit, etc.)
- Location and facilities
- History (if established)
- Products/services overview

**4. Market Analysis** (5-10 pages)
- Industry overview and trends
- Target market definition (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Market size and growth rates
- Customer segments (demographics, needs, behavior)
- Competitive analysis (direct, indirect, substitutes)
- Market positioning (differentiation)
- [Research: ...] citations throughout

**5. Organization & Management** (3-5 pages)
- Organizational structure (chart)
- Management team (bios, experience)
- Board of directors/advisors (if applicable)
- Key personnel requirements (hires, contractors)
- Governance model (decision-making, meetings)

**6. Products/Services** (5-10 pages)
- Detailed description (features, benefits)
- Unique value proposition (why customers buy)
- Product lifecycle (development, launch, growth)
- Intellectual property (if applicable)
- R&D activities (if applicable)

**7. Marketing & Sales Strategy** (5-10 pages)
- Go-to-market strategy (channels, tactics)
- Marketing channels (digital, events, partnerships)
- Sales process (lead gen, conversion, retention)
- Pricing strategy (benchmarked against comparables)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Lifetime value (LTV)

**8. Operations Plan** (5-10 pages)
- Production/service delivery process
- Technology and equipment
- Suppliers and vendors
- Quality control
- Facilities and location

**9. Financial Projections** (5-10 pages)
- Revenue model (3-7 streams with projections)
- 3-year financial projections (revenue, costs, profit)
- Cash flow analysis (monthly Year 1, quarterly Year 2-3)
- Break-even analysis
- Funding requirements (with 3-month reserve)
- Key assumptions (growth rates, conversion rates)

**10. Implementation Plan** (3-5 pages) - Palette addition
- 6-week action plan (week-by-week tasks)
- Milestones and checkpoints
- Resource requirements (time, money, people)
- Risk mitigation (what could go wrong)

**11. Appendices**
- Market research data (sources, reports)
- Comparable organization data (Form 990, financials)
- Product specifications
- Legal documents
- Resumes of key personnel
- Letters of intent from customers

**Customization by audience**:
- Investors: Emphasize financials, scalability, return potential, exit strategy
- Banks: Emphasize stability, collateral, repayment ability, cash flow
- Partners: Emphasize mutual benefits, capabilities, alignment, win-win
- Internal: Emphasize execution, milestones, resource needs, accountability
