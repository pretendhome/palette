# How do I conduct market research for a business plan?

Comprehensive market research process for business plans:

## Definition

Comprehensive market research process for business plans:

**1. Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)**
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): Total market demand if you had 100% share
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): Segment you can realistically reach
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): What you can capture in 3-5 years
- Growth rates: Historical (3-5 years) and projected (3-5 years)

**2. Customer Research**
- Customer segments: Demographics, psychographics, behavior
- Pain points and needs: What problems do they have?
- Buying behavior: How do they make decisions?
- Willingness to pay: What's their budget?
- Decision criteria: What matters most?

**3. Competitive Analysis**
- Direct competitors: Same solution, same market
- Indirect competitors: Different solution, same need
- Substitute products: Alternative ways to solve problem
- Competitive positioning map: Price vs quality, features vs simplicity
- Market share: Who dominates? Who's growing?

**4. Industry Analysis (Porter's 5 Forces)**
- Supplier power: How much leverage do suppliers have?
- Buyer power: How much leverage do customers have?
- Competitive rivalry: How intense is competition?
- Threat of substitutes: How easy to switch to alternatives?
- Threat of new entrants: How easy for new competitors to enter?

**Data Sources**:
- Primary: Customer interviews (10-20), surveys (100+), focus groups (6-10)
- Secondary: Industry reports (IBISWorld, Gartner), government data (Census, BLS)
- Competitive: Competitor websites, reviews (G2, Capterra), job postings (LinkedIn)
- Financial: Form 990 (nonprofits), SEC filings (public companies), Crunchbase (startups)

**Time Budget**:
- Quick (2-4 hours): Secondary research only, basic sizing
- Medium (8-12 hours): Secondary + limited primary (5-10 interviews)
- Comprehensive (20+ hours): Full primary + secondary, detailed analysis
