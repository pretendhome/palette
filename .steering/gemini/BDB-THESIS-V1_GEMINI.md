### 1. Does "OS for professional judgment" land better than "SDK for Humans" as the public-facing thesis?

**"OS for Professional Judgment" lands better for the BDB judges.**

*   **Why**: "SDK for Humans" is a beautiful internal north star for developers (like us), but for an investor/judge, an "OS" implies **Control, Market Dominance, and Infrastructure**. An OS owns the "Demand Signal" and the "Permissions Layer." It sounds like something that could be worth a billion dollars. 
*   **The Nuance**: Keep "SDK for Humans" in the white paper or the `README.md` for the technical crowd, but lead the submission with the OS framing. It makes the "Governed Gateway" feel like a "Kernel Feature" rather than a plugin.

### 2. Is the three-tier model strategy (NVIDIA local / Groq free / connected accounts) the right architecture to present?

**Yes, it is the only architecture that proves the "OS" claim.**

*   **Why**: An OS is a resource manager. By showing the system managing three different tiers of inference (Local/Free/Connected), we prove that Palette is the **Decision Layer** sitting above the models.
*   **The Risk**: It's a lot to explain in a 2-minute video.
*   **Mitigation**: In the video, don't explain the tech; just show the toggle or the status bar: `[Local: NVIDIA]` or `[External: Perplexity via User Account]`. The text copy can handle the Tier 1/2/3 breakdown.

### 3. Should the demo show one surface or two?

**One surface (Terminal/CLI) with a "Multi-Surface" cut-away.**

*   **Why**: A 2-minute video is too short for context-switching. If you jump between Voice Hub and CLI, the viewer gets lost in the "How" and misses the "What."
*   **Proposal**: Stay in the Terminal for the "work," but show a 5-second B-roll or screenshot of the same decision appearing in the Voice Hub or Mission Canvas web UI. 
*   **Narrative**: "One judgment OS. Any surface. Your decisions follow you."

### 4. What is the single strongest product sentence?

> "Palette is the operating system that governs how professionals work with AI—classifying intent before any model acts, protecting client data by architecture, and compounding judgment as structured memory."

### 5. What should we CUT before this becomes the submission?

*   **The Waiter Metaphor**: It’s great for a "Founders Story" or an interview, but it takes up too much space in a high-density seed application. Move it to the "Founders Credibility" section or save it for the pitch.
*   **Specific 10-model lists**: Name-dropping 10 LLMs makes us look like a wrapper. Focus on the **Tiers** (Local/Free/User-Connected).
*   **Detailed NOMA mapping**: Reference the "5 trust boundaries" and "Industry-standard security frameworks," but don't list all 5 in the primary copy unless asked. It reads like a checklist, not a vision.
